FRIENDLY GREETING AT THE GATE

Eiko's friend Mimoza is outside of Oeilvert, patiently waiting for your arrival. The moogle has a letter she'd like you to deliver to Mooel. Mimoza also runs a Mogshop, with a new weapon in stock for practically all of your characters! Her inventory is listed at the end of this section. The prices are kind of steep. However, you can find some of the items in the next few areas of the game, such as Steiner's Diamond Sword. On the other hand, you need to stock up on Softs right away, because there are enemies in Oeilvert that can petrify your characters at will, and there are other uses for this item.

EPITAPHS
keywork: EPIT01

One monster encountered inside Oeilvert is a powerful magic tomb called an Epitaph. Epitaphs attack your party several times, so it's good to study them. After two or three turns, an Epitaph opens itself, releasing a clone of one of the characters, which will attack the party. If it is a clone of a member of your current party, then the doppelganger will cast Death on its double and take him or her out of the fight.
If the doppelganger resembles another character not currently in your party, then it will use that character's abilities to attack your group, including magic if applicable. You can counter this by using a Soft on the Epitaph, which kills it instantly! Probably the only drawback is that you don't receive any EXP or Gil for this easy way out of the fight.

EXCELLENT EXP!

The Epitaphs are also great sources of EXP if you can kill off all three doppelgangers. The maximum it will spit out is three. If it releases a clone of one of your current characters, wait until all three doppelgangers are dead, and then revive your fallen character before finishing off the Epitaph. At this point, the Epitaph will only cast Break and Petrify spells, so if your characters have the Jelly support ability equipped, you'll have no problems.

THE COSMIC PUZZLE

Between the moogle and the front door, you're likely to have a random encounters with Ogres. At the front gate, the doors magically open by themselves.

In the first area, search under the platform in the back for a chest containing a Remedy. Go up the stairs and open the chest up top to find a Rising Sun. Save this for Amarant to throw during your next Boss fight.

Follow the top platform through a doorway to the left. Take the Elixir from the chest in the corner, and then examine the magic globe. This causes it to change mysteriously from blue to red, which gains more significance in a few moments as more story is revealed.

Return to the lobby, go back downstairs, and proceed through the lower doorway to the left. Pick up the Diamond Sword from the chest on the lower part of the screen. As you continue to the right, a holograph appears in the center of the room. This catches the group's attention for a few moments. Circle around behind the image and raid the chest for the Shield Armor. There are a few doorways to the far left; ignore them for now, and proceed through the exit at the bottom of the screen.

There are two projectors set into the floor on this platform. Examining them causes nothing to happen at the moment, though. In the chest to the far right is a Power Vest. Head up the steps to find two more floor projectors and Feather Boots in a chest nearby. As Zidane suspects, there is a certain order to activating the projectors. Start with the top-left one, and work your way clockwise down to the one nearest the entrance. This reveals a bit of the history of airships, but not the ones used in Gaia. Following this, head out the top exit to explore further.

Zidane must cross a series of arching bridges. In the center of the two spans, search the global object and a short slide show begins. Then continue across the bridge to the left. This exits back into the area with the large hologram, just outside the previously locked blue door. Inside this chamber, a host of spooky faces enlighten the party about the plight of another world.

With these clues firmly in hand, return to the lobby and continue through the door on the far right. Another moogle stands at the back of this chamber, the one named Mooel, for whom you should have a letter. Make sure that each character has the Clear Headed ability equipped, and save your game. As you proceed around the chamber to the right, another hologram appears in the center of the room. Pick up the Gaia Gear (an excellent piece of armor) in a chest near the door on the other side.

STILTZKIN'S BACK!

If you visit Mooel before entering the room of "spooky faces," you'll find Stiltzkin. He'll offer you another three-piece set of items.

Stepping through the final doorway, it seems you've reached a dead end. However, upon examining the platform, you activate an ancient mechanism that causes you to spiral down into a gigantic pit. At the bottom, Zidane finds the Gulug Stone. But something large and hostile shows up, attempting to prevent you from taking Kuja's prize.

ARK BOSS FIGHT TIPS

Freya's attacks are fairly weak, even when she performs a Jump and hurls her spear. Her best use in this battle is her skills, which are still operable in spite of the magic ban in Oeilvert. Have her cast Reis's Wind several times during the fight, because the Ark uses a wind attack that seems to blow away the Regen effect. Also, this wind attack will confuse any character who doesn't have the Clear Headed ability. This effect is removed when another character attacks them. Since you seriously risk killing your own party members by doing this, equip that ability beforehand!
The Ark also has a Photon attack that takes a character down to 1 HP. You can't do anything to avoid this attack, but keeping Reis's Wind in effect at all times will help keep your characters' HP from being depleted.
Have Amarant throw Pinwheel, Rising Sun, or Wing Edge weapons from your inventory. If you run out of them, have him throw duplicates of any weapon you have in stock, such as The Ogre or Exploda. Throwing weapons inflicts twice as much damage as Amarant's normal attacks, since the Ark levitates off the ground the entire time. The Ark's floating state also makes it difficult for Quina and Zidane to cause much damage. If you have Steiner in your party instead, his Power Break and Armor Break abilities will make it easier for everyone to attack.
Aside from the ability to confuse your party, the Ark provides little challenge. Continue pounding away at it until your characters begin to Trance, and then have them perform their most powerful assault to bring down the big ship. After the fight, take the Gulug Stone from the altar; this causes the scene to shift back to your comrades in captivity at the Desert Palace.
