CITY OF PERPETUAL RAIN

The rainy Burmecian Kingdom stands in ruins, the populace having fled in fear of the Black Mage Army. The dead or dying litter the streets, with little to offer but their eternal silence. Quickly move the group through the rainy area toward the back of the kingdom, searching for items along the way. Random encounters will be numerous and frequent, so be quick to keep you party from getting worn out before the finish.

In the first area, search behind the overturned cart to find a Cancer Stellazzio. Then proceed through the arch at the back. Zorn and Thorn are just steps ahead of you it seems. Once again, your group gets attacked by Black Mages, so hit them with each character's strongest attacks. When the jester twins retreat, head up the stairs to the left door.

The fountain in this foyer serves no purpose, but directly behind the stairs you can pick up a Soft. There's a treasure chest on the forefront of the third floor's balcony; however, it's in fact a Mimic monster. You can avoid these fights by not encountering the chests, but receive Ethers for defeating. During the fights, make sure you kill the Magic Vice it calls before it steals any of your inventory.

Proceed past the locked bell door to the western foyer. As you run toward the treasure chest on the left, a section of balcony drops below, bridging a gap. However, if you walk towards the treasure chest on the other side you will discover Germinas Boots inside. The balcony will then fall after attempng to cross back.

Run back through the other foyer and go outside. Cross under the balconies to the right-hand door, and grab the Potion from the chest. Continue up the stairs and head through the doorway on the second floor balcony. Take note that the chest in the foreground is another Mimic.

Angle your way through the next room and hop balconies to the bedroom. A dying Burmecian soldier points out that you can find the Protection Bell by searching behind the bed. Search behind the bookcase on the left to find an Ether as well. With the bell in hand, return to the bell door on the third level, and ring it to enter.

Running up a magnificent staircase, the party encounters fleeing soldiers who mistake Vivi for the enemy. Freya smoothes things over with Burmecian Soldier Dan so that you can proceed up the stairs to the temple area.

Go in the central bottom door to rescue some Burmecians who invite you to visit them in Lindblum. Keep that in mind for later in the game. Then exit and take the right stairs, crossing the balcony in front to the left doorway. Inside you'll stumble across two chests containing a Phoenix Down and a Tent. The chest on the far right side of the room is a you guessed right-Mimic!

In the courtyard under the royal castle, there are doors to the left and right. Enter the left one first, where Freya will claim the Mythril Spear held by the statue. Equip this weapon immediately, so Freya can cast Regen on all the party members at once. The right-hand door from the courtyard leads to a similar treasure room, where you can find a Lightning Staff for Vivi in the chest behind Atla the moogle. Atla will let you use a Tent and Save (both are recommended), and he sells a variety of good items too in a new feature called Mogshop. (A complete inventory is listed at the end of this section.)
   
          

MORE LETTERS FOR MOGNET
If you have a letter to give Atla from Mogki, then the moogle will give you a Kupo Nut. Atla also needs you to deliver a letter to Monev.

STILTZKIN IN BURMECIA
Stiltzkin enters as you retrieve the Lightning Staff. Stiltzkin offers to sell you some items if you speak to him. Even if the price seems unfair, purchase them anyway to keep Stiltzkin on his journey. On each occasion that Stiltzkin appears, he will sell you better and better items at more reasonable prices each time.

THE EVIL REVEALED

Seek out the moogle, save your game and use a Tent. Equip Zidane and Quina with the strongest weapons available, and equip Freya with the Mythril Spear and give Vivi the Lightning Staff so he can cast Thundara and Poison. Also, buy Vivi a Magus Hat from the moogle so you can cast Slow. If you thought Gizamaluke was tough, you haven't seen anything yet!

On the outside of the castle walls, Zidane has some choices about approaching Freya. Choose to leave her alone. The Dragon Knight then leaps up to the top of the castle walls, and leads the heroes inside. There, Zidane and Freya overhear a conversation between the Queen and a mysterious new villain. When a Burmecian soldier foolishly risks his life, Freya and Zidane intervene. As the Queen escapes, the superhuman General Beatrix threatens to end the adventures of our heroes.

Fighting this mortal goddess is quite a bit suicidal, but you must struggle to keep everyone alive until the end of the fight. Beatrix has attacks such as Shock and Thunder Slash, which may render a character KO'd in a single strike.
To make it easier for everyone to survive, Vivi should cast Slow and Freya should cast Reis's Wind in the first round. Zidane should use the support ability Bandit, so it will be easier to steal Beatrix's awesome items.
Afterward, Freya should Jump every round and Vivi should hit Beatrix with Thundara or Blizzara to cause around 550 HP of damage. Beatrix will target Vivi, so have Quina heal and resurrect him and the rest of your party with Potions and Phoenix Downs. If Quina can cast Mighty Guard, this should preserve everyone long enough for Zidane to steal all of Beatrix's items before she performs her devastating Stock Break attack. This attack reduces the entire party's HP to 1, and the battle ends.
