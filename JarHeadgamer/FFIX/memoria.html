WAR IN HEAVEN

A LITTLE HELP
In Memoria, exclamation marks will appear over Zidane's head in various corners and niches. If you press the action button to examine these areas, Zidane will shake his head in confusion. These areas are where you may battle a Card Master's ghost. Press the Square button to initiate a card battle with a ghost. Certain rare cards can only be obtained in this manner.

At first, you'll think you've made a terrible mistake. No matter how ready you think you are, you cannot be prepared for the spectacle that greets the Invincible at the gates of Memoria. Only with the aid of all the airships in the world can the Invincible land.

Memoria is populated with monsters so powerful, each battle is like a Boss fight. It's important to use Dagger's Scan spell on each new enemy you face. Pinpointing the enemy's weaknesses and exploiting them is paramount to survival in this surrealistic lair.

 NOVA DRAGON

NOVA DRAGON BOSS FIGHT TIPS
(keyword: NOVDR3)
HP:
54,940
AP:
 13
Weak Vs.:
 Ice, Wind
Steal Items:
 Grand Armor, Dragon Wrist, Remedy
Spoils:
 Wing Edge, Ether
Starting off with a Boss fight this tough is a bit disconcerting. My favorite party to use for this fight is Zidane, Dagger, Steiner, and Vivi. This is especially so after gathering all the awesome treasures from the chocobo side quest. However, if Quina has the Night spell to cast, then take Steiner out of the mix and equip everyone with the Insomniac ability.
First, use a Tent on the Nova Dragon, which may cause it to go blind. It may also cause all of its counterstrikes to miss. Armed with the Ragnarok, Steiner can knock down 9999 HP each round with the Shock ability. Dagger should summon Bahamut round after round.
Here's another nifty little trick to practice: Equip Vivi with the Black Robe and the Reflectx2 ability. Then have Dagger cast Reflect on herself, and aim all of Vivi's spells at her. They will bounce off and strike the Nova Dragon for double the normal amount of damage! Using the Flare spell with this technique, Vivi may cause about 9999 HP every round.
Also, equip everyone with the Insomniac ability, then have Quina cast Night at the end of every round. The characters will all attack, and then Quina will put the Nova Dragon to sleep before it can fight back!

CASTLE OF DREAMS

Zidane and the party now find themselves at the entrance of a place created entirely from their deepest fears and their long-forgotten pasts. Garland acts as a tour guide throughout the area when necessary.

Move up the brick path to the next screen. You'll notice that an icon appears near the entrance. If you want to leave, return to this spot and you can go straight back to the Invincible. Just ahead is a magic globe. These globes serve as save points throughout Memoria. There are also several hidden save points along the way. Each save point lets you save your game, use a Tent, or select new party members.

Continuing past the magic globe, you'll learn your first big lesson about exploring Memoria. As you search along the right wall, you'll slip into a hidden niche. Continue searching in here until you find Kain's Lance, which is Freya's strongest weapon. There are many top weapons for your characters hidden in places just off the path like this one.

Continue along the path until you reach a gothic area full of spires and arched window panes. There's a small platform you can explore at the top of this area. Search this platform until you find The Tower. This is a good weapon for Zidane, but there is an even stronger one available in the chocobo side quest.

CARD MASTERS
(keyword: CRDMST7)

There are five Card Masters lurking in Memoria. Here's where you can find them:

  • Master Phantom: He's hiding on the left side, right by the entrance in the "Stairs of Time" field. Press the Square button to challenge him.
  • Defense Phantom: He's hiding on the left side in the "Recollection" field.
  • Strong Phantom: In the lower-right corner, near some wall, in the "Lost Memory" field.
  • Rare Phantom: On the second floor in the "Time Warp" field.
  • Dark Phantom: Around the right side of the "Stairs" field.

 MALIRIS

MALIRIS BOSS FIGHT TIPS
(keyword: MALRS3)
As you start to ascend a long and curving bridge, Maliris attacks without warning. This is the first of several Bosses who closely resemble the Guardians of Terra.
HP:
59,497
AP:
 10
Weak Vs.:
 Ice
Steal Items:
 Masamune, Ultima Sword, Genji Armor
Spoils:
 Phoenix Pinion
Before entering this fight, equip all of your characters with the Body Temp ability. Keep this ability equipped for the next four Boss fights. Maliris uses Mustard Bomb often, and you've probably seen the deadly effect of this attack earlier in the game. The creature also casts various fire spells too, so protective armor will prove beneficial.
Have Dagger and Vivi team up to use his Reflectx2 ability to saturate Maliris with Blizzaga spells. Maliris will cast Reflect on itself anyhow, so if Vivi is in the party, then Dagger or Eiko will need to cast Reflect on themselves at some point no matter what.
Steiner can inflict 9999 HP with either the Shock ability or Blizzaga Sword. Dagger can pound the creature with Leviathan or Shiva, and Zidane desperately needs to steal the cool items this Boss carries. Quina can cause a lot of damage if s/he can use the Aqua Breath ability.
The most important thing about this battle is to keep everyone's HP high, because the creature will perform a deadly final attack that will cause around 1000 to 3000 HP of damage.

SONGS OF YESTERDAY

The next chamber presents the party with a reenactment of Alexandria's destruction. Afterward, step onto the platform to find a hidden save point and the Angel Flute. Place Eiko in the party for the upcoming Boss fight. Continue onward to the right when prepared.

RUNE CLAWS
(keyword: RNCLW3)
Memories and regrets continue to haunt those who experienced them. When you reach the area with the eye floating at the top of the steps, be advised that the Boss fight will occur once you ascend. Before you go, access the hidden area to the left to find the Rune Claws.

 TIAMAT

TIAMAT BOSS FIGHT TIPS
(keyword: TIAMT5)
HP:
59,494
AP:
 10
Weak Vs.:
 Ice
Steal Items:
 Grand Helm, Feather Boots, Blood Sword
Spoils:
 Wing Edge
Equip each party member beforehand with equipment that protects against or absorbs Wind damage, and make sure everyone has the Clear Headed ability ready.
Each round, have Vivi cast Meteor and let Eiko summon the Boosted Fenrir. Each of these spells should inflict in excess of 8000 HP each round. Steiner should perform Shock repeatedly, and it wouldn't hurt to have Dagger stay in the party and act as the party's medic. Or, Dagger may be able to finish off Tiamat by summoning Odin. If not, then Quina will surely finish the job by casting Earth Shake each round.
Ending this battle in a short amount of time is important. Equipping Auto-Haste on Vivi, Zidane, and Steiner seems to speed things up a bit. After three or four rounds, Tiamat will try to make the entire party float, after which it begins using Snort to knock characters out of the battle.
Continuing to fight becomes very difficult for the remaining characters. You can counteract this by having Dagger or Eiko cast Float on affected party members. This should cancel out the effect and bring them gently down to earth.

CLOUD CITY

Continue up and around a series of endless staircases, and then cross the platform to the stairway that goes up into the sky. The next chamber contains a twisting path that is hard to navigate. Somehow, your characters can move faster if you use the directional pad plus the Circle button instead of an analog stick. Adjust the angle you're moving toward several times to compensate for each twist.

A magic globe rests on a bridge beyond the twisting path, and you should rest up and save before continuing. There is an optional Boss ahead that may take several tries to defeat, if you're interested in obtaining more good items.

OPTIONAL BOSS
(keyword: OPTBOS7)
Walk under the waterfall into a room where Quina tries to swim. You can encounter the optional Boss we mentioned previously by searching in the hidden area behind the coral reef to the right. He will warn you, but if you ignore him the fight ensues. This is Hades, an optional Boss and the Synthesist of the gods. A strategy for beating him, and a list of which items he makes are contained in the Side Quests section.

As you continue up the stairs, you'll be surprised by another Boss, the Kraken.

 KRAKEN

KRAKEN BOSS FIGHT TIPS
(keyword: KRAKN0)
HP:
59,496
AP:
 10
Weak Vs.:
 Thunder
Steal Items:
 Glutton's Robe, Wizard Rod, Genji Helmet
Spoils:
 Phoenix Pinion
Equip armor that protects your characters from water or absorbs it. With the Body Temp ability equipped, no one will succumb to Freeze spells. If there's a character who can't equip Body Temp, then either Dagger or Eiko must quickly heal them with Esuna before the Kraken kills that person with a tentacle attack. But if no one can be frozen, the Kraken notices your defensive measures and starts casting a special Water spell at the entire party.
The Kraken has three targets: the body and its two tentacles. Hit the two tentacles until they cease to be targets. Pound the creature with Fire magic (Phoenix, Ifrit or Bahamut summons).

PERSISTENCE OF TIME

Proceeding past the Kraken's remains, Zidane enters an area where a strange clock displays an unreadable hour. There is a small niche to the right before going up the steps, where you can find a hidden save point. Save your game and go up the steps and through the door floating in space.

After an event, climb up the ladder and search the left side of the first platform. The Mace of Zeus is Vivi's final staff, and contains the Doomsday spell. This powerful spell attacks everything on-screen, and will dispose of your entire party unless you equip them all with armor or objects that protect against or absorb Shadow. Therefore, don't cast this spell indiscriminately!

Equip the Locomotion ability on all of your characters and continue to the room at the top. As you step forward, the room dissolves away to reveal its true form. Lich then salutes your party and commences attack.

 LICH

Lich performs devastating earthquake attacks to try to kill everyone at once. This undead lord also attempts to cast Stop on individual party members. Offset this with the Locomotion ability beforehand, or use a Remedy to cure the afflicted.
Again, have Dagger or Eiko cast Reflect on a party member and let Vivi bounce Flare off of them to cause 9999 HP damage per round. Steiner's Ragnarok sword seems to perform Critical hits like crazy against this Boss.
Also, have Eiko summon Madeen to cause some major damage. Quina can also pound this Boss by using the Twister spell (if you've learned it).

SPACE COWBOYS

After defeating Lich, check the left side of the room to find a hidden save point. Then step off the ledge into what looks like deep space. As Zidane floats, press up on the directional pad to direct him toward the top of the screen. You may encounter a random battle with a Behemoth in the process.

When you reach the light at the top, Zidane enters the Crystal World. The random monsters encountered here are all lesser versions of the four Chaos Guardians you just defeated, all made of crystal. They yield a high amount of AP, but no EXP.

Follow the crystalline path until it swoops upward in a semi-loop. Again, you must constantly adjust your thumb on the directional pad to compensate for the ever-changing forward direction.

Finally! You've reached the last platform of Memoria. A magic globe here enables you to rest, save, or select your party. An added feature enables you to teleport back to the entrance of Memoria. However, you cannot teleport back to this location from the entrance. If you leave, you must follow the same path all over again to return to this point.

When you cross through the barrier, you'll experience the Deathguise Boss fight. After that, you have an opportunity to leave and return before facing Trance Kuja and Necron, but there is no break between those two. Good luck!

 DEATHGUISE

This Boss resembles a giant version of Taharka that casts Meteor on the whole party. It has a defensive habit of closing itself up, during which time it only casts magic. Quina's Magic Hammer ability may prevent further casting of the Meteor spell.
Use Quina's Night trick: Equip everyone with the Insomniac ability before the battle, command everyone to attack, and then have Quina end each round by casting Night to put Deathguise to sleep. Perform this same tactic every round thereafter to prevent the Boss from counterattacking.
There should be other strategies at your disposal by now, such as Steiner's Shock attack or Vivi's Reflectx2 tactic casting Flare. Otherwise, this isn't an incredibly tough battle to live through compared to the fights to come.

 TRANCE KUJA

There is the opportunity to leave the final battleground and save your game in the previous room. Not using this chance is foolish, because none of the battles you've fought thus far can compare to what you're about to experience.
Choose your party wisely. The following are a few character-specific hints. Equip Freya with Kain's Lance and the High Jump ability. Use Amarant if you have a lot of Wing Edges and Rising Suns in stock. The skills he's learned, such as Auto-Life, will be of tremendous help. Steiner with the Ragnarok is also a treat!
Vivi fits in great because of his Reflectx2 ability. Quina is useful if s/he has eaten a lot of Blue Magic abilities. Eiko is actually the better of the two summoners to employ, because she can protect the entire group by calling Carbuncle. And then, of course, there's Zidane, whom we're counting on to Trance at least once during each battle.
Equip all of your characters with the following abilities, in order of priority: Auto-Life, Auto-Reflect, Auto-Regen, Auto-Haste, Counter, and High Tide. This will help protect them from dying constantly during these last two battles. Also, wear armor and pieces that provide a strong defense bonus against magic.
When the confrontation with Kuja begins, summon Carbuncle. Keep Ruby Light in effect (if you didn't equip Auto-Reflect) the whole time, recasting it every two rounds. This prevents damage from everything but Kuja's Flare Star attack. The madman can attack or cast spells every single turn! He casts Reflect on himself often, so spell casters like Vivi or Quina must bounce spells off fellow party members who are also protected by Reflect.
Use items or Quina's White Wind ability to heal every single round. Even if characters are just slightly low in HP, that could change during the next turn, so give Hi-Potions and Elixirs to everyone as needed.
After that, all we can tell you is to just pound away with your strongest attacks. If you can, have Eiko call Madeen to do some big damage to Kuja.
When the evil warlord realizes he is defeated, he will nearly kill everyone in the party. Just when the threat is supposed to be ended, a new menace emerges from the darkness at the end of eternity to destroy the world! Choose who will fight the final battle, and the other party members will sacrifice their life energies so that your HP and MP can be completely restored for the final showdown.

NECRON

The final Boss awaits. Equipping Jelly, Antibody, Clear-Headed, and Loudmouth will help prevent the myriad of status effects that Necron is capable of inducing all at once.

You must figure out ways to quickly cure the Mini status as well. We hope that this guide has helped you get the most out of the FINAL FANTASY IX experience, and that you enjoyed the game more thoroughly with our help. And now, enjoy the dramatic and well-deserved ending!
