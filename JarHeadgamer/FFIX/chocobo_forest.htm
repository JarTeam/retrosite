RIDING CHOCOBOS

The Chocobo Forest is an important place to visit before heading into Gizamaluke's Grotto. Learning how to ride Chocobos and playing the digging game is essential to many of the game's side quests.

Upon entering the Chocobo Forest, you meet the moogle Mene. Take the Gysahl Greens and exit the forest. Right outside and just south of the Chocobo Forest are some Chocobo tracks. Stand on the tracks, open the menu, and use the Gysahl Greens. You'll hear Choco go "Kweeehh!", and then Choco will appear. Press the X button to mount Choco, and use the D-pad to steer it. Ride up to the Chocobo Forest once again and press the X button to enter. Now Mene will explain how the Chocobo Hot and Cold game works, plus he'll sell Gysahl Greens to you for 80 Gil a piece.

There is also a mini-game called "Hot and Cold". For 60 Gil, you can ride a Chocobo around the small forest area and dig for items under the soil. More on this game is detailed in the Side Quests chapter.

CHOCOGRAPHS

There are certain items buried deeper beneath the ground in the forest called Chocographs. Chocographs are items that give clues as to the whereabouts of buried treasure that Choco can dig up on the World Map. During your first visit to the Chocobo Forest, you should be able to find the first Chocograph within 3-4 games. Labeled "Streamside," this Chocograph lets you dig up a treasure chest buried on the beach near the river just outside the Chocobo Forest.

To use a Chocograph to find buried treasure, leave the Chocobo Forest riding Choco. Press the Triangle button to open the Chocograph menu, select the treasure you want to search for, and exit the menu. Now toggle the Select button until the mini-map appears in the lower right-hand corner of the screen. The picture of the treasure's location should appear just above the mini-map. Ride Choco to that location and begin searching by pressing the Square button. Choco will make the same sounds as in the Hot and Cold game to indicate the proximity of the treasure. The Streamside treasure yields 2 Elixirs, 3 Hi-Potions, 4 Ethers, and 2 Germinas Boots.

Some Chocographs you find aren't useable until you proceed to new areas or new continents. It should be noted that some Chocographs aren't usable until Choco gains new abilities. You won't find others until you have the ability to command a boat or an airship.

Chocographs also enable Choco to grow, changing into a blue or red Chocobo. Different colored Chocobos have different abilities, such as swimming, climbing mountains, or flight. If you invest the time and the Gil to collect all 20 Chocographs, then you'll receive the Gold Chocobo! More on Chocographs and the different colored Chocobos is detailed in the Side Quests chapter.
